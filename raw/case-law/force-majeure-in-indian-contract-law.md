# Force Majeure in Indian Contract Law


Force majeure is a common clause in contracts that frees both parties from liability when an extraordinary event occurs. Under Indian law, Section 56 of the Indian Contract Act deals with the doctrine of frustration, which is the closest equivalent to force majeure.

The Supreme Court has held that force majeure clauses must be narrowly construed. COVID-19 led to a surge in force majeure invocations across commercial contracts.

- Indian Contract Act, 1872, Section 56
